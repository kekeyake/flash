우리 작업은 새로운 패러다임을 위한 실제 건물을 탐구하는 서구 도시의 변화와 개발 움직임 등과 같이 시작되었다. 변화는 도시 공공 공간의 점진적인 민영화에 의해 이루어졌으며, 이는 현대건축에 지대한 영향을 미쳐왔다. 공공 자금 부족으로 도시와 지역 당국은 도시계획을 적극적으로 진행하기가 불가능해져 투자가들에게 공공 공간을 매각하였으며, 이에 투자가들은 도시에서 가장 크고 중요한 부분을 개발하였다. 이는 그 목적이 쉽게 예측되는 하나의 게임으로, 결국 건축은 세계 경제의 이익을 극대화하는 인프라가 될 것이다.

Wolf D. Prix

Born in 1942 in Vienna, Austria, Wolf D. Prix was educated at the Technical University of Vienna, the Architectural Association of London and at the Southern California Institute of Architecture (SCI-Arc) in Los Angeles. Since 1993 he has been the Professor of the Architecture 3 Masterclass at the University of Applied Arts in Vienna, Austria. From 1999 to 2003 he was Dean of Architecture, Industrial Design, Product Design, Fashion Design and Stage Design at the same University. Since October 2003 Mr. Prix holds the position of Vice-Rector for Space and Quality as well as the position of apread of the Institute of Architecture' at the University of Applied Arts in Vienna. In 1984 he was a visiting Professor at the Architectural Association in London, England, and in 1990 a visiting Professor at Harvard University in Cambridge, Massachusetts.


  From 1985 - 1995 Wolf D. Prix served as an Adjunct Professor at the Southern California Institute of Architecture (SCI-Arc) in Los Angeles, California. Since 1998 he has been a faculty member at Columbia University in New York City. In 1999 Mr. Prix held the Harvey Perloff Professorship at the University of California Los Angeles (UCLA). In 2001 he was an Adjunct Professor at UCLA. Since 2001 Wolf D. Prix has been a Doctor Honoris Causa de la Universidad de Palermo, Buenos Aires, Argentina.

  From 1995 to 1997 Mr. Prix held a position as an Architectural Counselor to the Federal Ministry of Science, Research and the Arts.
From 2000 to 2006 he is a member of the Austrian University Curatorial Committee. Wolf D. Prix is a permanent member of the Austrian Art Senat as well as of the European Academy of Sciences and Arts, headquartered in Vienna, Austria. He is also a member of the Architectural Associations of Austria, Germany and Santa Clara, Cuba.

  Helmut Swiczinsky

  Born in 1944 in Poznan, Poland. Helmut Swiczinsky was raised in Vienna, Austria and educated at the Technical University in Vienna, Austria, and the Architectural Association in London, England. In 1973 he served as a visiting Professor at the Architectural Association in London, England.
Helmut Swiczinsky is a permanent member of the European Academy of Sciences and Arts, headquartered in Vienna, Austria.

  Wolfdieter Dreibholz

  Born in 1941 in Vienna, Wolfdieter Dreibholz was educated at the Technical University of Vienna from where he received the degree Dipl. Ing in Architecture in 1966. From 1968 to 1974 he was an assistant with the Faculty of Architecture at the Technical University of Graz in Styria, Austria, from where he graduated in 1977 with a Doctor of Technology degree. From 1978 to 1998 Mr. Dreibholz worked with the Planning Department of Styria, which he was appointed Director of in 1990. In this capacity he was responsible for all public building projects including universities, office buildings, schools, housing and etc.
In 2000 Wolfdieter Dreibholz joined Coop Himmelb(l)au as CEO of Coop Himmelb(l)au Mex S.A. de C.V. and became Partner of Coop Himmelb(l)au Prix & Swiczinsky & Dreibholz ZT GmbH, of which he became CEO in 2004.

  Mr. Dreibholz is a member of the chamber of architects of France and Austria and the author of articles and research papers in the fields of architecture and urbanism.
