KPF_ Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates Firm Description

  Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates is an international architectural studio committed to providing the highest quality architecture which responds to its environment in terms of context and sustainability. KPF's design philosophy is firmly rooted in the belief that success is the result of collaboration and dialogue, and that it is their responsibility to make informed contributions to the built environment.
The work of the practice has been widely exhibited and honored worldwide, winning much critical acclaim. The firm has received more than 200 awards for design excellence and has been the subject of nine monographs. The firm has 17 partners, and its staff of over 350 people is drawn from 43 countries, speaking 30 languages. The partners believe that the different backgrounds of the staff help to broaden the firm's vision.
  KPF is organized around the concept of collaboration, both within its studios and through its associations with some of the world's leading figures on the built environment. A single integrated team works on the project through all phases until completion of construction. Team members sit together in open plan spaces. The firm structure is informal and the office has no closed doors.
The central concern of the practice is design excellence. Since its foundation in 1976, KPF has focused on building in the city. Each building has a civic responsibility above and beyond its specific function to respect the tradition of its place. The design of each building draws from its context; in turn, it becomes part of the fabric of the city upon which future generations can build.
KPF's buildings have a responsibility not only to the city around them and to those who work in them, but also to the environment at large. Today, society is more aware that the world's resources are not limitless, and that we must work to limit the world's pollution. KPF's work acknowledges these concerns through designs that use renewable sources of energy, relying on passive systems to reduce the need for energy and reducing pollutants released into the air.

William Pedersen _ FAIA FAAR

  William Pedersen is the principal Design Partner of Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates, the firm which he founded in 1976 along with A. Eugene Kohn and Sheldon Fox. KPF has earned the Architectural Firm Award by the American Institute of Architects (1990) and the Gold Medal of Honor by the New York Chapter of the AIA (1989).
  Mr. Pedersen has received five National Honor Awards from the AIA for the New Academic Complex, City University of New York/Baruch College (2003), the World Bank in Washington D.C. (1998), Westendstrasse 1/DG Bank Headquarters in Frankfurt, Germany (1994), the Procter & Gamble World Headquarters in Cincinnati (1987) and 333 Wacker Drive in Chicago, Illinois (1984). Other major projects include the World Financial Center in Shanghai, the Gannett/USA Today Headquarters in Virginia and the New York Sports & Convention Center in Manhattan.
  Mr. Pedersen received the Gold Medal for lifetime achievement in architecture from Tau Sigma Delta, National Architectural Honor Society. Additional honors include, among others, the Arnold W. Brunner Memorial Prize in Architecture for Contributions in Architecture as an Art (1985), awarded by the American Academy and the Institute of Arts and Letters; the 1990 University of Minnesota Alumni Achievement Award and the Rome Prize in Architecture (1965), awarded by the American Academy in Rome.

A. Eugene Kohn _ FAIA RIBA JIA

  A. Eugene Kohn currently serves as the Chairman of Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates. In 1976, along with William Pedersen and Sheldon Fox, he founded KPF based on a commitment to design excellence. Since then, Mr. Kohn has served as Partner-in-Charge of many of KPF's major domestic and international projects and is responsible for many of the firm's new commissions.
Eugene Kohn is respected worldwide, not only for his 40-year career as an architect, but also for his inspirational leadership qualities. As Founder and Principal of KPF, he has developed a global strategy and has shaped the firm into one of the world's leaders in all aspects of the profession of architecture.
  In 1987, Mr. Kohn was honored by the National Education Fund and, in 1995, by the Sheltering Arms Children Service. In 1996, Mr. Kohn was honored with the Sidney L. Strauss Award from the New York Society of Architects, as well as in 1997 with the Lifetime Achievement Award given by the Wharton Real Estate Centre. Mr. Kohn was recognized in 1998 with the Ellis Island Medal of Honor. Among the many honors bestowed on Mr. Kohn, he is an Executive Fellow of the Graduate School of Design at Harvard University and is the first architect to be awarded that title.
  Mr. Kohn is a Fellow of the American Institute of Architects and a member of its Octagon Society. During 1988, Mr. Kohn held the position of president of the AIA New York City Chapter. He is also a member of the Royal Institute of British Architects, the Japan Institute of Architects, and Honorary member of the Fellows of the Philippine Institute of Architects. Mr. Kohn also served as a Trustee for the University of Pennsylvania's Graduate School of Fine Arts and the Wharton Real Estate Center Advisory Board.

Lee A. Polisano _ FAIA RIBA

  Lee A. Polisano is the president of Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates and founding partner of KPF's London studio, Kohn Pedersen Fox International. Under Mr Polisano's direction, the firm's work addresses a number of primary concerns, namely a response to context, an awareness of the civic obligation of buildings, respect for the environment and the importance of technology.
Mr. Polisano's work emphasizes the civic and ecological responsibility of the built environment. Projects such as the innovative World Trade Center in Amsterdam and the large-scale urban regeneration master plan for the City of Glasgow have been widely recognized for achieving these goals. His headquarters for Rabobank in the City of London - Thames Court - was awarded The British Council for Offices' National Award for Best Urban Workplace for 1999, and a Civic Trust Commendation. This project has been widely credited with transforming commercial architecture in the city of London.
  Mr. Polisano has published several articles on a range of subjects in Europe and North America, and is frequently invited to address academic and professional symposia. He was the first recipient of Virginia Polytechnic Institute's Outstanding Professional Accomplishment Award and delivered the 125th Anniversary Distinguished Alumni Address to the University. In 1999, Mr. Polisano was elevated to The College of Fellows of the American Institute of Architects because of his "notable contributions to the advancement of the profession of architecture."
