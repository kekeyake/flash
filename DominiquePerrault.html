Dominique Perrault
 Dominique Perrault
 - Architectural geo-couturier






1. Biography
 

1953

ㆍBorn in Clermont-Ferrand, France

1973

ㆍEnters school of architecture and frequents the office of Martin van Treck

1978

ㆍDiploma in architecture (Ecole Nationale Supeieure des Beaux-Arts de Paris)

1979

ㆍHigher diploma in town planning (Ecole supeieure des Ponts et Chaussee, Paris)

1980

ㆍPostgraduate degree in history (Ecole des Hautes Etudes en Sciences Sociales, Paris)

1981

ㆍOpening of his office in Paris and first built work: factory Someloir in Chaeaudun, France

1983

ㆍWinner of the ‘Programme for New Architecture’(PAN XII)

1984

ㆍFirst notable project: ESIEE (academy for engineers of electronics and electrical engineering) for the Chamber of Commerce and Industry of Paris - Marne-la-Vallee

1989

ㆍPostgraduate degree in history (Ecole des Hautes Etudes en Sciences Sociales, Paris)

1990

ㆍCompletion of the Hotel Industriel Jean-Baptiste Berlier in Paris. As its first tenant Dominique Perrault is setting up his office to build the National Library. The building receives the ‘l'Equerre d'argent’ in 1990 and the ‘Constructa Prize’ in 1992.

1992

ㆍWinner of the international competition for the Velodrome and Olympic Swimming Pool in Berlin, Germany
ㆍOpening of an office in Berlin

1993

ㆍGreat National Prize of Architecture, France

1995

ㆍInauguration of the French National Library by Francois Mitterrand on March 23

1996

ㆍWinner of the international competition for the town hall in Innsbruck, Austria

1997

ㆍMies van der Rohe Pavilion Award for European Architecture, conferred by the Foundation Mies van der Rohe and the European Parliament for the French National Library
ㆍFirst commissioned project: factory Aplix in Cellier-sur-Loire, France, which receives the ‘World Architecture Award’ in Hong Kong, first prize for best industrial building in 2001

2000

ㆍOpening of an office in Luxemburg for the extension of the Court of Justice of the European Communities in Luxemburg

2002

ㆍWinner of the international competition for the New Olympic Tennis and Multisports Center, Madrid, Spain
ㆍOpening of the town hall in Innsbruck, Austria
ㆍWinner of the competition for the Urban planning of Donau-City centre, Vienna, Austria

2003

ㆍWinner of the international competition for the New Building of the State Academic Mariinsky Theatre, St. Petersburg, Russia

2004

ㆍWinner of the international competition for the New EWHA Womans University Campus Center Project, Seoul, Korea

1996

ㆍSpecial mention, Constructec-Prize 1996, (European prize of industrial architecture) for the water-processing plant SAGEP

1990

ㆍFirst prize AMO (architect/client) for the Hotel Industriel Berlier
